# Google Business Profile + Local Citations Checklist

For a home care agency, **Google Business Profile (GBP)** is the single biggest local‑ranking factor, ahead of anything on the website. This is the off‑site work that makes the on‑site work (your county and new city pages) actually rank. Do the GBP section first; it moves the needle fastest.

We're working **region by region, starting with Central** (Anne Arundel, Baltimore City/County, Howard, Harford, Carroll). Focus your reviews, posts, and citations on Central clients and towns first, then repeat the same playbook for the next region.

---

## 1. Claim & verify (do first)

- [ ] Sign in at business.google.com and **claim "My Home Cares."** If a listing already exists, claim it rather than making a duplicate.
- [ ] Complete **verification** (postcard, phone, or video). Nothing ranks until you're verified.
- [ ] If any **duplicate** listings exist, request their removal (duplicates split your reviews and confuse Google).

## 2. Core profile (exact consistency matters)

Your **NAP** — Name, Address, Phone — must be **identical everywhere** online. Pick one exact format and never vary it:

> **My Home Cares** · [your street address], Glen Burnie, MD 21060 · **(410) 231‑3076**

- [ ] **Name:** "My Home Cares" (don't stuff keywords like "My Home Cares Home Care Baltimore" — that risks suspension).
- [ ] **Primary category:** **Home health care service**. Add relevant secondary categories: *Home help service agency, Nursing agency, Aged care.*
- [ ] **Phone:** (410) 231‑3076 (same number as the website).
- [ ] **Website:** https://www.myhomecares.com/
- [ ] **Service area:** Since you visit clients, set it to a **service area business** and list your Central towns (Glen Burnie, Annapolis, Severn, Pasadena, Baltimore, Towson, Dundalk, Columbia, Ellicott City, Bel Air, Westminster…). Google lets you list up to 20 areas.
- [ ] **Hours:** Set accurately; add a note that phone support is available for urgent needs.

## 3. Fill everything out (completeness = ranking)

- [ ] **Description (750 chars):** who you serve, that you're a licensed Maryland RSA (RSA‑01229 | HCSA‑00845), services offered, and the areas you cover. Write naturally.
- [ ] **Services:** add each service as its own item with a short description — Skilled Nursing, Personal Care, Companion Care, Respite Care, Homemaking, Meal Planning, Alzheimer's & Dementia Care, Parkinson's Care, 24‑Hour/Live‑In Care. Link each to the matching page on your site.
- [ ] **Attributes:** "Identifies as veteran‑owned/woman‑owned" if applicable, "Online estimates," "Language(s) spoken," etc.
- [ ] **Photos:** add 10+ real photos — caregivers with clients (with consent), your team, your logo, exterior/office. **Add a few new photos monthly** (active profiles rank better).
- [ ] **Q&A:** seed 3–5 common questions yourself (Do you accept Medicaid? How fast can care start? What areas do you serve?) and answer them.

## 4. Reviews (the biggest ongoing lever)

Volume, recency, and your responses all influence ranking. This is where Central‑first focus pays off:

- [ ] Set up your **review short link** (GBP → "Ask for reviews" → copy link). Then send it to me and I'll wire the site's review buttons to it directly.
- [ ] Ask **every satisfied Central family** for a Google review — in person, by text, and in a follow‑up email. Aim for **1–2 new reviews per week**.
- [ ] **Respond to every review** within a day or two — thank positives, address negatives calmly and professionally. Never include client health details in a reply (HIPAA).
- [ ] Encourage reviewers to mention their **town and the service** naturally ("companion care for my mother in Severna Park") — those keywords help local ranking.

## 5. Google Posts (free visibility)

- [ ] Post to GBP **weekly** — a tip, a service highlight, a hiring post, or a seasonal note (e.g., the Medicaid renewal reminder). Posts show in your listing and signal an active business.

---

## 6. Local citations (NAP consistency across the web)

Citations are mentions of your exact **Name, Address, Phone** on other sites. Consistency builds trust with Google. Get listed on (free tiers exist for most):

**Core aggregators & directories**
- [ ] Google Business Profile (above)
- [ ] Bing Places for Business
- [ ] Apple Business Connect (for Apple Maps)
- [ ] Yelp
- [ ] Facebook (Page → verify address/phone)
- [ ] Better Business Bureau (Maryland)

**Healthcare / senior‑care specific**
- [ ] Caring.com
- [ ] SeniorAdvisor.com
- [ ] A Place for Mom (provider listing)
- [ ] Care.com (business profile)
- [ ] Home Care Association resources / MNCHA member directory (you're a member)

**Local / Maryland**
- [ ] Maryland Department of Aging / local Area Agency on Aging resource lists (Anne Arundel, Baltimore, Howard, Harford, Carroll)
- [ ] Local chamber(s) of commerce (Anne Arundel, Baltimore County)
- [ ] Nextdoor (business page)

**Rules for every citation**
- [ ] Use the **exact** same Name, Address, Phone, and website every time.
- [ ] Use the same category ("Home health care service") where offered.
- [ ] Link to https://www.myhomecares.com/ (or the relevant city page).

---

## 7. Region‑by‑region cadence (our operating rhythm)

1. **Now — Central:** GBP fully optimized, service area = Central towns, reviews from Central families, citations built, weekly posts. Track results in the demand dashboard + Search Console.
2. **After Central gains traction:** repeat the exact checklist emphasis for the **Capital region** (Montgomery, Frederick, Prince George's) — update GBP service areas, gather reviews from those clients, add region‑specific citations.
3. **Then** Southern, Western, Eastern Shore in turn.

Don't spread reviews and citations thin across all of Maryland at once — concentrate them where you're actively serving, and the local pack rankings follow.
